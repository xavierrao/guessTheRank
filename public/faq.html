FAQ & How to Play

Everything you need to know

⚡ Quick Start
  1. Enter your name and create or join a game
  2. Share the Game ID with friends
  3. The host starts the round
  4. Read your question and drag players into your ranked order
  5. Guess where a random player ranked someone else
  6. Scores are revealed — repeat!
How do I start a game?
Enter your name on the home screen and click "Create Game". Share the Game ID with your friends — they enter their name and the ID to join. Once everyone is in, the host clicks "Start Game".
How many players do you need?
The game works best with 3 or more players. With only 2 players the ranking mechanic becomes too simple.
How does ranking work?
Each player gets a unique "Who is most likely to..." question and secretly ranks all players from most likely (#1) to least likely. Drag the player tiles into your preferred order, then submit.
How does guessing work?
After everyone ranks, one player's ranking is revealed at a time. A random target player is chosen and everyone (except the ranker) guesses what position the ranker placed that target. Pick a number from #1 to however many players there are.
How are points scored?
Guessers earn 1 point for correctly identifying the exact rank position. The ranker earns 1 point for each correct guess, up to a maximum of 3 points per reveal.
Can the ranker guess?
No — the ranker sits out the guessing phase for their own reveal, since they already know the answer. They earn points based on how many others guess correctly.
What happens if the game goes inactive?
Games are automatically deleted after 1 hour of inactivity. If this happens you will be returned to the home screen.
Can I rejoin if I leave?
Yes — if you close the tab or navigate away, you have a 15-second window to return. Navigate back to the game URL or re-enter your Game ID. Your name is saved automatically in your browser.
Where do the questions come from?
Questions are generated in real time using AI (via the Groq API). Each question is checked for similarity to previous ones to keep things fresh. If the AI is unavailable, the game falls back to a built-in question pool.
